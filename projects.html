Non-contact rail wheel defect diagnostics using air-coupled Ultrasonic Acoustic Emission (MxV Rail)

Air-coupled UAE (20–80 kHz), physics-based validation, multi-class ML diagnostics.
Use the arrows or trackpad to scroll.

This research, conducted in collaboration with MxV Rail, advances a non-contact diagnostic framework for detecting structurally significant rail wheel defects using air-coupled Ultrasonic Acoustic Emission (UAE). Unlike traditional contact-based methods that require liquid couplants or low speeds, this approach captures high-frequency (20–80 kHz) ultrasonic bursts generated by defect-related frictional energy dissipation under dynamic loading.

By establishing a physics-based ground truth through controlled impact testing, the project identifies a robust acoustic fingerprint that enables wheel health assessment without interrupting revenue service. This physical foundation is further elevated by an automated machine learning framework, where multi-class classifiers translate these complex acoustic signatures into precise, actionable diagnostic insights, bridging advanced mechanical sensing with data-driven AI solutions.

  • Non-contact sensing at operationally relevant speeds and standoff distances.
  • Controlled experiments to validate defect-specific acoustic signatures.
  • Multi-class ML pipeline for diagnostic inference and feature interpretability.
